2. SHORT HEAD BICEPS EXERCISE

The short head of the biceps brachii is one of the two muscle heads of the biceps, located on the inner side of the upper arm. It originates from the **coracoid process** of the scapula and merges with the long head to form the biceps muscle. The short head primarily contributes to **elbow flexion**, **forearm supination**, and shoulder stabilization. Exercises that target the short head include **preacher curls**, **concentration curls**, and **wide-grip barbell curls**, as these emphasize the inner biceps. Strengthening this muscle improves arm aesthetics and functional strength for pulling and lifting movements.

(The red part of the muscle)

1. Concentration curl

Studies have shown that supination and flexion can be used to increase short head activation. Therefore, starting in a neutral position and finishing in a supinated position will target the short head. Concentration curls are an excellent way to maximally activate the biceps. By resting the elbow against your leg, they also have the advantage of minimizing movement and thus activation of other muscles.

3. BRACHIALIS BICEPS EXERCISE

The brachialis is the muscle that is most responsible for bicep width… And it’s often sorely neglected in our bicep training routine!

1. Hammer curl

A regular hammer curl is inadequate for hitting the brachialis because if you involve any sort of supination while bending the elbow you’re going to shift the focus over to the biceps and away from the brachialis.
